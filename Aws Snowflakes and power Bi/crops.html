Connecting the Dots: Key Relationships

Synthesizing the data reveals critical insights: cooler temperatures are strongly linked to higher yields, and high rainfall doesn't always guarantee a bumper crop.

Rainfall vs. Yield: More Isn't Always Better

There is no direct correlation between higher rainfall and higher yields. Locations like Kodagu and Mysuru achieve top yields with moderate rainfall, while high-rainfall Bangalore sees only average results. This highlights the importance of water efficiency over sheer volume.

Temperature vs. Yield: The Cooler Advantage

An inverse relationship is clear: the cooler Rabi season has the highest yields, while the warmer Kharif and Zaid seasons are less productive. This suggests heat stress may be a significant limiting factor for many crops in the region.
